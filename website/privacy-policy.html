📋 Overview

1. Overview & Introduction

PalliCare ("the Platform", "we", "us") is a palliative care management software developed by AFO . This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect information when you use the PalliCare platform — including our mobile apps (Android and iOS) and web dashboard.

By using PalliCare, you agree to the practices described in this Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the platform.

ℹ️

This policy applies to all users of PalliCare including administrators, healthcare workers, volunteers, and any organization onboarded to the platform. Patient data entered by your organization is subject to both this policy and your organization's own data governance obligations.

This policy is governed by applicable Indian data protection laws including the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA) and other relevant regulations.

📦 Data Collection

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account & Organization Information

  • Organization name, address, and contact details
  • Administrator name, email address, and phone number
  • User credentials (username/email and securely hashed passwords)
  • User roles and access permissions within the platform

2.2 Enquiry & Contact Information

  • Name and contact details submitted via our enquiry/demo request form
  • Organization details and patient volume estimates provided during onboarding enquiries
  • Messages and communication records with our team

2.3 Usage & Technical Data

  • Device information (device model, OS version, app version)
  • Log data (IP address, timestamps, actions performed)
  • App usage patterns and feature interactions
  • Error and crash reports for debugging

🏥 Healthcare Data

3. Sensitive Healthcare Data

⚠️

PalliCare processes sensitive personal and medical information. Healthcare organizations using this platform are responsible for ensuring they have appropriate consent from patients or their legal guardians before entering data into the system.

Healthcare data processed through PalliCare may include:

  • Patient names, addresses, and contact information
  • Medical history, diagnoses, and care plans
  • Home visit records, clinical notes, and observations
  • Medicine distribution and supply records
  • Equipment supplied to patients and their return status
  • Volunteer assignment and coordination logs
  • Deceased patient records (retained for audit purposes)

As the Data Fiduciary, your organization determines the purpose and means of processing patient data. AFO acts as the Data Processor, processing data solely on your instructions and for the purposes of operating the PalliCare platform.

We will never use patient data for advertising, analytics products, or any purpose beyond providing and improving the PalliCare service.

⚙️ Data Use

4. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information for the following purposes:

  • Platform Operation: Providing, maintaining, and improving the PalliCare software
  • User Authentication: Verifying user identity and managing role-based access
  • Customer Support: Responding to enquiries, troubleshooting issues, and providing onboarding assistance
  • Communications: Sending service updates, maintenance notices, and support communications
  • Security: Detecting and preventing unauthorized access, fraud, and abuse
  • Legal Compliance: Meeting our obligations under applicable law
  • Product Improvement: Analyzing aggregated, anonymized usage patterns to enhance features
✅

We do NOT sell your data. We do NOT use patient data for marketing, profiling, or any commercial purpose outside the PalliCare service agreement.

🔗 Sharing

5. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal or patient data. We may share data only in the following limited circumstances:

5.1 Service Providers

We work with trusted third-party service providers who assist in operating the platform (e.g., cloud hosting, email delivery). These providers are contractually bound to process data only on our instructions and maintain appropriate security standards.

5.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or government authority — particularly where failure to disclose would violate applicable Indian law.

5.3 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or asset sale involving AFO, user data may be transferred as part of that transaction. You will be notified in advance.

5.4 With Your Consent

We may share information with third parties when you have explicitly consented to such sharing.

We will never share patient healthcare data with advertising networks, data brokers, or any party outside the scope of providing the PalliCare service.

🔐 Security

6. Data Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organizational measures to protect your data:

  • Encrypted Transmission: All data in transit is protected via HTTPS/TLS encryption
  • Secure Storage: Data at rest is stored in encrypted, access-controlled databases
  • Role-Based Access: Strict permission controls limit who can view, edit, or delete records within your organization
  • Authentication: Secure login with session management and token-based APIs
  • Regular Backups: Automated, tested backups to prevent data loss
  • Audit Logs: Comprehensive logging of access and modifications for accountability
⚠️

No system is 100% secure. In the event of a data breach affecting your organization, we will notify you promptly in accordance with applicable legal requirements.

⏳ Retention

7. Data Retention

We retain data for as long as necessary to provide the PalliCare service and meet our legal obligations:

  • Active Account Data: Retained for the duration of the service agreement
  • Patient Records: Retained per your organization's retention policy; we provide data export on request
  • Enquiry/Contact Data: Retained for up to 2 years for business communication purposes
  • Log & Technical Data: Retained for up to 12 months for security and debugging
  • After Account Closure: Data is deleted or anonymized within 90 days unless legal obligations require longer retention

You may request early deletion of your data by contacting us. Note that some data may need to be retained for legal or audit purposes.

✋ Your Rights

8. Your Rights Under DPDPA 2023

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, and other applicable law, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of personal data we hold about you
  • Right to Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of personal data, subject to legal limitations
  • Right to Grievance Redressal: File a complaint if you believe your data rights have been violated
  • Right to Nominate: Nominate another person to exercise rights on your behalf in case of death or incapacity
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for non-essential data processing at any time

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the details in Section 12. We will respond within 30 days of receiving your request.

📋

For patient data rights, requests should generally be directed to the palliative care organization that manages the patient relationship, as they are the Data Fiduciary for that information.

🍪 Cookies

9. Cookies & Tracking

The PalliCare web dashboard uses limited, essential cookies to operate the service:

  • Session Cookies: Keep you logged in during a session (deleted on logout)
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and display preferences
  • Security Tokens: Prevent cross-site request forgery (CSRF)

We do not use advertising cookies, social media tracking pixels, or third-party analytics that share data with external advertising platforms.

The marketing website (this page and the landing page) may use basic analytics (e.g., anonymized page view counts) to understand how visitors engage with our content. No personal data is linked to this analytics.

👶 Children

10. Children's Privacy

PalliCare is not directed at individuals under 18 years of age for account registration purposes. We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors for account creation.

The platform may contain patient records of minors receiving palliative care. Such records are managed by the healthcare organization with appropriate consent from the child's parent or legal guardian, as required by law.
